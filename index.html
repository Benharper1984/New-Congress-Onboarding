New Congress Onboarding Guide

Welcome to New Congress! Please review the expectations and agreements below, which help us maintain a respectful, comfortable, and cooperative living environment. We value open communication and flexibility, and encourage everyone to contribute to making this house a home. Thanks for being part of this shared space—we’re glad you’re here.

This guide outlines our community's values, expectations, and systems to help new tenants and work traders feel supported and integrated into the household.

1. Community Values

  • Communicate Openly: Express your needs and listen to others respectfully.
  • Be Respectful: Honor boundaries, differences, and each person's space.
  • Be mindful of how your behavior may be affecting others
  • Be Flexible: Compromise when needed and support solutions that serve the group.
  • Be Responsible: Clean up after yourself and follow through with commitments.
  • Set Boundaries: Know and express your limits kindly.
  • Be Inclusive: Embrace diversity in politics, race, religion, lifestyle, etc.
  • Have Fun: Enjoy living in community and take opportunities to socialize!

2. Quiet Hours

  • Inside: Mon–Fri: 10PM–8AM | Sat–Sun: 11PM–8AM
  • Outside: Mon–Thu: 10PM–8AM | Fri–Sat: 12AM–8AM
  • Exceptions allowed if approved by affected members.
  • Be mindful of noise in the kitchen at night.

3. Chores

We use a rotating chore system for weekly and monthly upkeep.

Bathrooms

  • Weekly: Sweep, clean sink/toilet, replace hand towels
  • Monthly: Deep clean tub, tile, mirrors, mop
  • As needed: Unclog drains, empty trash, declutter

Living Room

  • Weekly: Water plants, sweep
  • Monthly: Wash towels, vacuum and mop, dust bookshelves, clean under furniture
  • As needed: Tidy porch, fold blankets, organize

Kitchen

  • Weekly: Clean counters/table, sweep, clean sink, wipe freezer bottom
  • Monthly: Deep clean stove, oven, mop, clean side porch
  • As needed: Wash stray dishes, remove expired food

4. Kitchen Etiquette

  • Leave the kitchen cleaner than you found it
  • Wash dishes right away. If you are busy and need to come back to them then put them on the left side of the sink and put your name tag on them acknowledging they are yours. Clean within 24 hours
  • Don’t leave dishes in the sink!
  • If someone cooks for you, help clean
  • Keep dining table clear

5. Trash, Recycling, Compost

  • Ben takes trash and recycling bins to the road, but everyone is responsible for taking full bins outside.
  • If a bin (including compost) is full past the lid, take it out.
  • Compost: Use pitchfork to dig a hole, dump compost and cover it with leaves
  • Do not put compost in the trash!

6. Parking

  • We have 1 parking spot in the driveway and 2 on the street in front of the house.
  • We can street park in front of the uphill neighbor’s house (up to 2 cars), but don't block their fence gate.
  • We can park just past our fence in front of the downhill neighbor’s house, but not in the second spot further downhill (it blocks the neighbor across the street and they will throw a fit).
  • Additional parking is available at the bottom of the hill.

7. Pets

  • Coordinate care for pets left over an hour.
  • Clean up hair and toys; scoop poop daily or every couple days if they poop in the designated area up the hill.
  • You will be charged $5 per poop pickup for any poops left in paths for over 2 days
  • Pet owners are liable for damage, behavior, and ensuring their pets’ needs are met.
  • Do not feed pets in shared spaces.
  • Keep toys out of public spaces; maintain clear paths.

8. House Expectations

  • Respect others and avoid disrespect or discrimination
  • Keep noise down during quiet hours unless cleared with others
  • Keep personal items in your room or out of communal spaces. Get consent if you'd like to leave or store anything anywhere outside of your room.
  • No hard drugs in the house (includes unprescribed opioids)
  • Smoking limited to backyard
  • Respect privacy and go above minimum expectations

Additional Guidelines for Tenants

Month-to-Month Lease Terms

  • All tenants are on a month-to-month lease, offering flexibility for both the tenant and the household.
  • A minimum of 15 days’ notice is required from either party to end the lease, but more is encouraged.
  • Rent is due on the first of each month, unless another arrangement has been made.
  • Lease terms and rent amount can be renegotiated monthly with mutual agreement.
  • Any proposed changes (e.g. rent increase, policy adjustments) will be communicated at least one month in advance.
  • Failure to meet lease terms or uphold community agreements may result in a warning or a request to vacate, with a minimum of one week’s notice depending on circumstances.
  • Submit rent on time (amount and method shared during onboarding).
  • Help onboard new members and maintain house systems.
  • Guests (over 2 nights/week) must be approved.
  • Responsibility includes upkeep of private and common areas.

Additional Guidelines for Work Traders

  • Expected to contribute the agreed number of hours per week in exchange for housing and in some cases food.
  • Tasks may include cleaning, organizing, gardening, repairs, or special projects.
  • Log your hours and communicate with your point person.
  • Be reliable—communicate early if you can’t meet a deadline or shift.
  • Respect household systems and participate in communal life.

Conflict Resolution

At New Congress, we aim to handle conflicts with empathy, respect, and open communication. If tension arises, tenants are encouraged to reflect first, then talk directly using "I" statements. If needed, housemates can help mediate or the matter can be brought to a house meeting. If expectations remain unmet, the house manager may step in to help find a resolution. We believe conflict, handled well, can strengthen our community.

Power Dynamics & Shared Accountability

We recognize that having either the house owner or a designated house manager living on-site can introduce unique power dynamics. While this person may carry certain responsibilities, all tenants are encouraged to communicate openly and honestly—regardless of perceived authority. Everyone deserves to feel safe voicing concerns or giving feedback.

As the owner, I have put over 1,000 hours of work into improving this house and continue to make enhancements nearly every day. I ask for patience and understanding when a space is temporarily messy due to a project in progress. Help is always welcome but never expected—and any support you do offer directly improves the home you live in.

The house manager (which may be the owner) is committed to shared accountability and being receptive to respectful input. Tenants are encouraged to bring up concerns directly or request a mediator if helpful. Creating a healthy community means addressing issues together, not avoiding them.

If you ever feel uncomfortable sharing feedback directly, you are welcome to do so via a trusted housemate or at a house meeting. We all play a role in shaping a fair and caring home.

Final Notes

By living here, you agree to uphold these community agreements. If you cannot meet these expectations, your month-to-month lease may not be renewed or in extreme cases be evicted before the lease is up.

We’re excited to have you here. Let’s build something beautiful together!
